# Customer Segmentation Analysis

## Overview
I analyzed a dataset of 2000+ customers to identify groups for targeted marketing. I have used K-means clusteringn to find 4 clear segments based on income, demographics, and spending habits.

## The 4 Customer Segments:

## Cluster 0: Low Spending Digital Browsers
* Financials: The lowest income ($31,084) and spending ($99) segment.
* Preferences: They visit the website more frequently than any other group.
* Demographics: The youngest average age (37) and 80% are parents.
* Strategy: Use digital first marketing and entry level discounts.

## Cluster 1: High Income Premium Shoppers
* Financials: The highest average income ($81,203) and the highest total spending ($1,626).
* Preferences: They are the top buyers of wine and meat product. They have a 60% response rate to campaigns.
* Demographics: They live in small households on averag e (avg 1.8 people) and are rarely parents (13%).
* Strategy: Focus on premium product offerings and loyalty retention. They are already highly engaged and willing to spend.

## Cluster 2: Price Sensitive Large Families
* Financials: Moderate income earners ($48,930) with conservative spending habits ($362).
* Preferences: This group uses more coupons and deals than any other cluster.
* Demographics: 100% of this group are parents, and they have the largest household sizes (avg 3.27).
* Strategy: Market toward this group using bulk discounts, family promotions, and coupons.

## Cluster 3: High Income Multi Channel Shoppers
* Financials: Rich segment with an average income of $70,726 and high spending ($1,172).
* Preferences: They have been customers for the longest period and shop frequently across all channels like web, store, and catalog.
* Demographics: Middle aged customers with mid sized households.
* Strategy: Maintain their loyalty through multi channel engagement and personalized rewards based on their diverse shopping history.
